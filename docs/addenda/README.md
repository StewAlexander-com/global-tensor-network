Supports the repo claim that NH₃ is an efficient hydrogen carrier on the `energy_density → ammonia` edge.

Headline figures (all linked to primary sources inside the PDF):

- **12.7 MJ/L** liquid NH₃ vs **8.49 MJ/L** liquid H₂ — 50% more chemical energy per litre.
- **121 vs 71 kg-H₂/m³** — 1.7× more hydrogen per cubic metre.
- **−33 °C at ~10 bar** vs **−253 °C at 1 bar** for LH₂ — ~220 °C warmer liquefaction.
- **~0.5 kWh/kg** vs **10–12 kWh/kg** liquefaction energy per kg-H₂ payload.
- **~180 Mt/yr** produced via Haber–Bosch; **150+ ports** already handle NH₃ at scale.

Sources cited in the PDF: ACS Publications / Energy & Fuels (2023), Patsnap (2026), Heise/IFA (2024), ARIEMA (2025).

Headline figures (all linked to primary sources inside the PDF):

- **12 / 14** — energy density touches every other node except AI · minerals.
- **Σw = 46** — the highest hub coupling total in the 62-edge graph (avg 3.83 / 5).
- **Two w=5 edges** — oil & gas (transport dominance) and ammonia (the H₂ carrier fix).
- **Stocks + Flows** — Meadows leverage rank #3 of 12; carrier chemistry rewires infrastructure.
- **59.1%** of global electricity still from fossils (Ember 2024); road-EV share only 4 → 17% by 2030 (IEA).
